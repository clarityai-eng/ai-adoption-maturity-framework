# AI Adoption Maturity Framework

This repository contains the full qualitative interview framework and scoring rubric developed by the **Clarity AI Engineering team** to benchmark and measure AI adoption maturity across our organization.

## Motivation

We created this framework to move beyond the hype cycle and establish clear metrics on the real-world impact, friction points, and risk surface of integrating AI tooling into the software development lifecycle.

Our goal was to answer critical questions about the shifting engineering role—specifically, the transition from **manual coders to orchestrators**—and quantify the resulting validation burden.

## Core Components

The framework is a set of interview questions and an accompanying rubric used to conduct deep-dive interviews with engineering and data science squads.

### The 8 Dimensions of AI Development Maturity

The qualitative scoring rubric assesses teams on a scale of **1 (No Adoption)** to **5 (Advanced/Optimized)** across eight key areas:

* **Adoption Level:** Frequency of use (Daily vs. Ad-hoc).
* **Tooling & Ecosystem:** Integration of professional tools vs. fragmented personal setups.
* **Skill & Prompting:** Depth of understanding—moving from basic iterations to systematic workflows.
* **Quality Control & Governance:** How to ensure AI does not degrade quality standards.
* **Process Integration:** How AI fits into the Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC), from discovery to deployment.
* **Team Alignment:** Shared norms vs. individual behavior.
* **Champions & Knowledge Sharing:** The presence of pioneers who scale practices.
* **Impact & Outcomes:** Observable gains in speed and quality.

## How to Use

1.  **Run Deep-Dives:** Use the provided interview template to facilitate qualitative discussions with engineering teams. We interviewed 19 teams, including Frontend, Backend, Data, SREs, Data Science, and Machine Learning Engineers.
2.  **Score and Benchmark:** Apply the scoring rubric to the interview findings to assign a maturity level (1-5) for each of the 8 dimensions.
3.  **Identify Gaps:** Use the results to pinpoint specific areas of silent divergence, skill atrophy, or validation burden that require a more prescriptive AI strategy within your organization.
